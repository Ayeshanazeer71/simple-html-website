About Ramadan

Ramadan is the ninth month of the Islamic calendar and is observed by Muslims worldwide as a month of fasting, prayer, reflection, and community.

History of Ramadan

Ramadan commemorates the revelation of the Quran to Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him). The night when the first verses of the Quran were revealed is called Laylat al-Qadr (The Night of Power).

Significance

  • Spiritual Purification
  • Self-discipline
  • Empathy for those in need
  • Community bonding
  • Family gatherings

Daily Schedule During Ramadan

  • Suhoor - Pre-dawn meal
  • Fajr Prayer
  • Fasting during daylight hours
  • Iftar - Breaking the fast at sunset
  • Taraweeh prayers at night
